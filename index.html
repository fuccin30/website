Featured Research

Vacancies After Visa Determinations: Evidence From Online Job Postings and H-1B Lotteries

Working paper | Sole author

This paper studies the impact of high-skilled immigration on firms and workers in response to the H-1B visa lottery. I construct firm-level measures of lottery success using administrative H-1B data and link these measures to detailed job posting and LinkedIn profile data. I find that firms with more favorable lottery outcomes increase vacancy postings in the months following the lottery, consistent with H-1B workers complementing rather than substituting for domestic labor.

Industrial Robots and Union Representation: Implications for Labor Market Outcomes

Working paper | Sole author

I investigate the differential impact of exposure to industrial robots on the earnings of union and non-union workers from 1983 to 2022. I develop a measure of occupational exposure to industrial robot technology using natural language processing applied to occupation taxonomies and U.S. patents. The results show that union members in robot-exposed occupations experience a significant earnings premium relative to non-union workers in the same occupation. In my preferred specification, a one standard deviation increase in exposure to industrial robot technology is associated with 2.6% higher weekly earnings for union workers.
